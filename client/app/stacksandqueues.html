All About Stacks

Stacks are data structures that operate using LIFO (last in first out) ordering. Meaning when you pop (remove) an item from a stack - you will receive the most recently pushed item. I like to think of this like a stack of papers, if you pile papers on top of each other and then go to retrieve one, you'll receive the most recently added paper (the paper on top of the stack).

Some notes on time complexity: adding and removing takes place in constant time. However, unlike arrays accessing elements at specific indices does not take place in constant time.

All About Queues

Queues are data structures that operate using FIFO (first in first out) ordering. So, you remove items in the orderer they were added in. The best way to think of this is just like a line at the bank. Usually, people are removed from the line in the order they arrived (except for that one time that lady cut me while I was tying my shoes, but the story of why I always wear flip flops to the bank is a story for another day).
